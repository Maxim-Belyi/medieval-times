meet the queen

Her Majesty Doña Maria Isabella bears the scepter and reigns supreme for the first time in Medieval Times’ history. The Queen oversees the tournament, judging who is worthy of earning the title: Defender of the Throne.


Before the show, take part in a knighting ceremony with the Queen. Long may she reign!

The Brave Knights

The Green Knight

A master of weaponry, the Green Knight of León is gifted in handling all weapons from the crossbow to the battle axe. The Green Knight lives up to the reputation as one of the most powerful knights of his kingdom.

The Red & Yellow Knight

As the fiercest swordsman in the Kingdom, the Red & Yellow Knight hails from Perelada to the northeast. From the first moment he picked up a dagger to train, the Royal Court knew he would bring glory to his people. Now, this agile fighter leaves everyone in awe with his swift swordwork.

The Red Knight

The Red Knight is a passionate warrior, earning him the name “Lion’s Heart.” He proudly wears the crest of the lion, for he is driven to bring honor to his people across the vast lands of Castilla. The Red Knight exemplifies courage and valor. The Lion’s Heart indeed.

The Blue Knight

The Blue Knight is renowned as a champion of the defenseless! Raised in the alleyways of Valiente, the Blue Knight grew to be strong and swore an oath to guard his fellow citizenry. The gallant protector rides into battle for the House of the Valiant, emblazoned with the fleur-de-lis, a symbol of righteousness.

The Yellow Knight

The Yellow Knight is an untamable force. Be assured, he lifts up the most vulnerable but those who dare to challenge him shall face his wild wrath. He was raised in the northern region of Nevarre, where herders are known to run bulls through the streets. That same untamed energy courses through the veins of the Yellow Knight.

The Black & White Knight

There’s no gray area for the Black & White Knight. He must win. Losing is not an option. He’s the mightiest in skill among an order of warrior priests whose arrival upon the field brings despair to theimpure of heart.

training for combat

The life of a knight at the castle is one of hard work and dedication. They train for years to master horsemanship and weaponry, always focused on safety first.

Every knight learns intricate tournament tactics using a variety of authentic weapons including the lance, axe, bola and a variety of swords. Precision and form are essential, not just for battles, but also to win the Medieval Games of Skill you’ll see during the tournament.

Through daily rigorous training and practice with our magnificent horses, the knights enter each tournament with pride and honor.
